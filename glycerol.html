Making Glycerol Stocks of Bacteria

  • Glycerol
  • Sterile water
  • Cryovials
  • Inoculated bacterial broth

1. Make up 50% glycerol solution with sterile water. You will need 500μl per cryovial.

2. Label all cryovials with contents, date, and initials.

Label and make cryovials for all colonies you have grown in liquid broth - you don't yet know which population expresses more of your desired plasmid.

3. Dispense 500μl of 50% glycerol solution into each cryovial.

4. Dispense 500μl of inoculated bacterial broth into each cryovial.

5. Freeze cryovials at -80°C in a Mr Frosty freezing container.

6. Glycerol stocks can be kept for years, though freezing and thawing cycles can decrease shelf-life.

7. To grow bacteria from frozen stocks, scrape frozen bacteria off the top and streak it as described in step 3 of bacterial colony isolation.
