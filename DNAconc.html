Measuring DNA concentration using a Nanodrop Spectrophotometer

  • Purified plasmid DNA
  • Nanodrop Spectrophotometer
  • 10μl pipette and tips
  • DNA solvent (e.g. elution buffer, for use as a blank control)
  • Dry paper towels

1. Turn on the Nanodrop and its software. Leave the arm down while it calibrates. Click "nucleic acids" on the first software menu.

2. Wipe the metal contacts of Nanodrop with a dry paper towel.

3. Pipette 2μl of clean DNA solvent onto the bottom metal contact. Close Nanodrop arm, press blank on the software.

This measures the absorbance of the DNA solvent without DNA, to use as a baseline measurement.

4. Once the blank measurement has been made, lift the Nanodrop arm and wipe down both metal contacts with a dry paper towel.

5. Pipette 2μl of your DNA sample onto the bottom metal contact of the Nanodrop. Close the top Nanodrop arm, press measure on the software.

6. Note the concentration of DNA in your sample that the Nanodrop has calculated.

The Nanodrop returns a few different ratios of the sample's absorbance of different wavelengths. Here's how you interpret them:
  • DNA concentration: The concentration of DNA in your sample. Double check what units it is measured in.
  • 260/280: The purity of DNA relative to RNA. A pure DNA sample should be around 1.8, pure RNA around 2.
  • 260/230: The presence of non-nucleic acid contaminants. Should be above 1.7.
