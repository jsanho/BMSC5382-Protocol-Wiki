Making and Pouring an Agarose Gel for DNA Electrophoresis

  • Agarose powder
  • 1X TAE buffer
  • Casting tray, well comb, casting blocks, gel box
  • SYBR-safe
  • Microwave
  • Heatproof gloves

1. Add 0.7g of agarose powder per 100ml TBE buffer to make 0.7% agarose solution.

100ml of agarose solution should be sufficient for at least 2 full gels.

2. Watching closely, microwave the agarose solution with the flask lid loosened. As soon as it boils, remove it from the microwave with heatproof gloves. Swirl to mix.

A loose flask lid prevents pressure build up. Flasks from the microwave must be handled with heatproof gloves.

3. If the swirled agarose solution is not homogenous, return it to the microwave and heat again until it boils. Swirl to mix, and repeat this step until solution is homogenous.

4. Leave the agarose solution to cool down on the side. While it is cooling, rinse the gel comb, casting tray and gel box with distilled water and leave them to dry.

5. When the agarose solution is cool enough to hold with bare hands (55°C), add SYBR-safe to 0.005% (5μl in 100ml agarose solution, for example). Swirl until thoroughly mixed.

SYBR-safe (or ethidium bromide) is a DNA stain that allows migrated DNA to be visualised with UV light following electrophoresis.

6. Assemble casting tray with casting blocks and gel comb. Pour agarose solution to just below the top of the teeth of the well comb.

Assembly of gel casting tray and pouring of agarose solution for DNA electrophoresis. Ensure well comb is at the negative end of the tray.

7. Allow the gel to set for 20-30 minutes at room temperature, or 10-15 minutes at 3-8°C.

Gently touch the corner of a gel to check if it has set; set gels are firm and cool to the touch.
