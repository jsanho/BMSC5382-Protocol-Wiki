Preparing and Pouring Agar Plates

  • LB Agar powder
  • 25ml stripette tips
  • 10cm Petri dishes
  • Sterile water
  • Cell culture hood
  • Selection antibiotic
  • Distilled water

Before beginning this protocol, check what form your selection antibiotic is in. If it is powder, make a 50mg ml-1 stock solution in distilled water (double check recommended concentration manufacturer instructions, this is a general guideline). Syringe filter the solution to sterilise it.

Steps beginning with this icon should be done in a cell culture hood.

1. Add 3.7g of LB agar per 100ml distilled water in an autoclavable flask. Prepare at least 100ml agar solution to produce 3 agar plates.

Ensure the water and agar mixture in the flask is no more than three-quarters of the total flask volume so it doesn't overboil.

2. Autoclave the flask. Handle with heat proof gloves following autoclaving.

3. Before the autoclaved flask is collected, preheat a water bath to 55°C.

4. Place the molten agar in the preheated water bath.

If the flask has cooled and the agar has solidified, carefully microwave it (with the lid loosened) using heat proof gloves until it begins to boil, then immediately take it out and swirl the flask. Repeat this process until it is fully molten and homogenous again.

5. Stripette 25ml of sterile water into a 10cm petri dish. Mark the level on the outside with a permanent marker, then empty the dish. Label all plates with date, initials, and contents.

This lets you know what level to pour the agar to. Empty and air-dry the dish inside the laminar flow hood to prevent contamination afterwards.

6. When your molten agar reaches the temperature of the water bath (it will be cool enough to hold without gloves), add your selection antibiotic to the correct concentration and swirl to mix.

7. Pour agar to the line previously marked on the petri dish. Pour additional plates to the same level.

Pour all 3 plates to the same level as the first, as determined by the reference line.

8. Replace the lids on all plates and leave them at room temperature for half an hour, or until completely set.

9. Store agar plates upside down at 3-8°C.
